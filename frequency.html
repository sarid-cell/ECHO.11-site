What is a binaural beat?
Two slightly different tones — one per ear. Your brain detects the difference and creates a third tone that doesn't physically exist. This is called a binaural beat. It's an auditory illusion with measurable neurological effects, first documented by physicist Heinrich Wilhelm Dove in 1839.
Why 10Hz?
10Hz sits in the Alpha wave band (8–13Hz) — the brain's natural state of relaxed alertness. Alpha is associated with reduced anxiety, enhanced creativity, and the quiet between stimulus and reaction. It's where the mind is present, but not reactive.
What happens in the brain?
The brain synchronizes its electrical activity to the frequency it perceives — a process called neural entrainment. As you listen, your dominant brainwave frequency gradually shifts toward 10Hz. The cortex quiets. Stress markers decrease. Attention sharpens without effort.

Research inspiration · Declassified 1983 / Released 2003

In 1983, the US Army commissioned an analysis of The Monroe Institute's research into binaural frequencies and altered states of consciousness. The resulting document — Analysis and Assessment of Gateway Process — was classified, then declassified and released by the CIA in 2003.

The report concludes that binaural beat technology can alter consciousness in measurable, reproducible ways — reducing cortical noise, shifting brainwave activity, and creating conditions for what the document calls "expanded awareness."
