What is a binaural beat?
Two slightly different tones — one per ear. Your brain detects the difference and produces a third tone that doesn't physically exist. An auditory illusion with measurable neurological effects, first documented by physicist Heinrich Wilhelm Dove in 1839.
Why 10 Hz?
10Hz sits inside the Alpha band (8–13Hz) — the brain's natural state of relaxed alertness. Alpha is associated with reduced anxiety, creative clarity, and the quiet between stimulus and reaction. Present, not reactive.
What happens in the brain?
The brain synchronizes to the frequency it perceives — a process called neural entrainment. As you listen, your dominant brainwave frequency shifts toward 10Hz. Cortical noise decreases. Attention sharpens without effort.
Can it help with anxiety?
Alpha-band entrainment is associated with reduced anxiety and a calmer nervous system — the quiet between stimulus and reaction. ECHO.11 was born from exactly that need: a free tool for acute anxious moments, insomnia, and racing thoughts, available 24/7. It is a self-care practice, not a medical treatment — if anxiety is disrupting your life, please also talk to a professional.
